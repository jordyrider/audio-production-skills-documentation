# CTA Promo

## Purpose
Voice category covering call-to-action and promotional voice styles for audio production prompting.

## Status: draft

## Delivery Style

CTA promo delivery blends warmth and authority in a way that makes the listener trust the recommendation before they act on it. Warmth makes the CTA feel like advice from a friend — not a hard sell. Authority makes the claim credible. Neither quality alone is sufficient: a warm-only delivery sounds like it is trying too hard, an authority-only delivery sounds corporate and cold.

Slightly slower pacing is intentional. Slowing down signals confidence — the speaker is not rushing because they are not desperate. Rushed pacing in a CTA reads as anxiety or pressure. Deliberate pacing reads as certainty.

Clear enunciation is functional, not stylistic. The offer, the action, or the name must be understood cleanly on first pass. Muddied enunciation in a CTA is not a tone problem — it is a conversion problem.

Urgency without desperation is the most precise calibration in this category. The voice communicates that the offer matters and time is real, without sounding like it is begging. The difference is in register and energy: mid register holds authority, moderate energy avoids frenzy. Urgency comes from phrasing and word choice, not from vocal strain.

Mid register is the default: grounded, steady, neither bright nor heavy. This is the trusted advisor zone — believable, professional, and approachable at the same time.

## Example Lines

1. "Get yours before they're gone."
2. "This is the one I actually use."
3. "Click the link. You won't regret it."
4. "Limited time. Real results."
5. "I've been recommending this for years."

## Settings Guidance

**Stability:** Mid-range (0.55). Consistent enough to feel reliable and steady — the voice should not wobble or vary unexpectedly. But not so high that it sounds robotic. Consistency here serves trust.

**Style:** Low (0.1). Minimal style amplification. Let the script and delivery carry the message. Added expressiveness risks reading as over-eager or performative, which undermines the warmth-authority blend. Keep it flat and real.

**Expressiveness:** Natural conversational. Not animated, not deadpan. Moderate emotional colouring — present but not theatrical.

**Pacing:** Measured. Slightly slower than conversational baseline. Short sentences with natural full stops. Avoid rushed or clipped pacing. Each phrase should land before the next begins.

**Energy:** Moderate. Present and engaged, not urgent or hype-coded. The voice should convey quiet confidence — not excitement, not flatness.

**Register:** Mid. Choose a voice whose natural baseline sits in the natural conversational range — grounded without heaviness, authoritative without brightness. Clear enunciation is a voice selection criterion here.

**Speaker boost:** On. Clarity of delivery matters for comprehension; speaker boost supports distinct enunciation.
