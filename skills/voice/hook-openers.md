# Hook Openers

## Purpose
Voice category covering hook opener voice styles — delivering the opening line that makes the viewer stop scrolling.

## Status: draft

## Delivery Style

Hook opener delivery is high energy with high expressiveness. The voice should sound like it is discovering something in real time — reactive, spontaneous, not scripted. This is the opposite of measured: the listener should feel the speaker's excitement before they understand what the content is about.

Non-uniform pacing is a deliberate choice, not a deficiency. An even rhythm signals rehearsed delivery; broken, lurching pacing signals genuine reaction. The voice should accelerate through excitement and pull back at moments of revelation. "ohh wait... ohh NOW this" only works if the pacing breaks where the text breaks.

Register sits mid-to-high. High register reads as bright and energetic without being shrill — the goal is youthful, forward-leaning intensity, not volume. The voice should feel like it is leaning toward the listener.

High expressiveness signals authentic emotion. If the expressiveness is too low, hook lines flatten into statement delivery and lose their scroll-stopping power. The voice needs pitch swings wide enough to carry surprise, curiosity, and excitement across a two-second clip.

Energy here is intensity, not loudness. A high-energy delivery can be at normal conversational volume. The intensity is in the forward-lean: urgency, discovery, reaction.

## Example Lines

1. "This is my favorite... ohh wait this, oh no wait this, ohh... oh now THIS is my favorite."
2. "You're telling me it can do this? This? And THIS?!"
3. "Everyone says to do this, buuuut... we didn't!"
4. "Nobody talks about how hard it is to go from this to this"
5. "We just finished this and... I'm kind of obsessed!"
6. "I wish someone told me this sooner"
7. "If you're doing this, stop! Do this instead"
8. "don't do it, don't do it, don't do it, ughhh"
9. "What's the one piece of advice that changed your life?"
10. "Okay but why is this actually working??"
11. "Not me realizing this just now"
12. "This wasn't the plan, but here we are"
13. "Tell me why this makes sense?"

## Settings Guidance

**Expressiveness:** High. This is the most expressiveness-heavy category in the set. Wide pitch variation and strong emotional colouring are correct here. If the output sounds animated or theatrical, that is the target — not a problem to solve.

**Stability:** Low (around 0.3). Lower stability means more variability between takes and more variation across syllables. For hook openers this is a feature: it produces the lurch and pull of reactive delivery. Consistent, even output is the failure mode here.

**Style:** Moderate (0.4). Enough style amplification to push the emotional colouring without making it artificial. Do not suppress style here — this category needs it.

**Register:** Mid-to-high. Choose a voice whose natural baseline sits bright. There is no parameter for register on ElevenLabs — voice selection is the register lever.

**Pacing:** Vary. Do not smooth out uneven pacing. Hooks live in rhythm breaks, not rhythm. Write ellipses, dashes, repetitions, and fragments into the script — these force pacing variation at the model level. Even pacing is the anti-pattern to avoid.

**Energy:** High. Forward-leaning, intense, discovery-mode. Not a narrator — a reactor.
