# Meme Quotes

## Purpose
Voice category covering meme and internet-culture quote voice styles for audio production prompting.

## Status: draft

## Delivery Style

Meme-quotes is the most stylistically variable category in this set. The correct delivery target depends entirely on the format — there is no single "meme voice." The two poles are deadpan and exaggerated, and both are deliberate creative targets.

**Deadpan** is not bad delivery. For dry humour, ironic format memes ("Me, a normal person: this is fine"), and understated internet observations, flat and even delivery is the correct output. Low expressiveness here is the joke — the gap between the absurdity of the text and the total absence of affect in the voice is the comedic mechanism. Do not attempt to make deadpan delivery "more expressive." That breaks the format.

**Exaggerated** delivery is correct for absurdist comedy, reaction memes, and high-energy format content. Wide pitch swings, animated inflection, and theatrical timing are all valid and useful here.

**Timing is the craft element.** In meme-quotes content, the joke lives in the rhythm, not the words. The setup and punchline structure depends on the pause landing in the right place. Pause *before* the punchline, not after — the held beat builds anticipation. A post-punchline pause deflates. Write timing into the script using dashes, ellipses, and colons.

This is the only category where voice selection matters as much as settings: a naturally dry voice will produce better deadpan than any settings adjustment to an animated voice. Choose the voice baseline to match the format first, then dial.

## Example Lines

1. "Me, a normal person: this is fine."
2. "Nobody: — Me at 2am:"
3. "POV: you remembered the thing you said in 2014."
4. "Them: relax. Me, internally:"
5. "Current mood: send help."

## Settings Guidance

**Deadpan target:**
- Stability: High (0.65–0.75). Consistent, even, unwavering delivery. Stability suppresses variation — which is exactly what deadpan needs. Variation breaks the flat affect.
- Style: Low (0.0–0.1). No emotional amplification. Suppress expressiveness. Let the text carry all the irony.
- Expressiveness: Flat. The expressiveness range should be minimal. Deadpan is deliberately low-expressiveness.

**Exaggerated comedy target:**
- Stability: Low (0.25–0.35). Lower stability allows wider variation between syllables and more dynamic pitch swings. This produces the animated, character-voice quality comedy takes need.
- Style: High (0.5–0.7). Amplify the emotional colouring. Let the voice lean into the absurdity.
- Expressiveness: Wide range — animated to exaggerated. Theatrical pitch swings are correct.

**Pacing:** Timing is the primary lever in this category. The pause before the punchline is more important than any parameter setting. Write it into the script. Short bursts, held beats, and abrupt stops are all meme-format tools.

**Voice selection note:** Choose the voice baseline before adjusting settings. A flat, dry voice baseline produces deadpan with minimal effort. A character-coded or animated voice baseline produces comedy takes more naturally. Settings amplify the direction the voice already goes — they do not reverse a voice's natural character.
