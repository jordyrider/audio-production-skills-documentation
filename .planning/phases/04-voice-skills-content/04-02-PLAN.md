    Body structure and required content per section:

    **## Register**
    - Define register as the perceived pitch placement and tonal weight of the voice
    - Range: low register (chest, gravitas), mid register (natural conversational), high register (bright, youthful, light)
    - How to specify register in Layer 1 terms: describe it as "low register", "bright", "warm mid-range", "authoritative chest voice" etc.
    - Translation note: On ElevenLabs, register goals are achieved via voice selection — choose a voice that matches the target register baseline. There is no parameter to shift pitch. On MiniMax, use the `pitch` parameter (±12 semitones) to adjust baseline register on top of the voice's natural pitch.

    **## Pacing**
    - Define pacing as the speed and rhythm of word delivery
    - Distinguish: overall tempo (words per minute), syllable density (rushed vs spacious), and rhythmic variation (even vs dynamic)
    - Layer 1 descriptors: "deliberate", "measured", "rushed", "staccato", "drawn out", "clipped", "flowing"
    - Pacing is a primary lever for perceived confidence and emotional weight

    **## Energy**
    - Define Energy and explicitly distinguish it from Volume: energy = emotional intensity and urgency, not loudness
    - Low energy = calm, subdued, understated. High energy = urgent, intense, forward-leaning.
    - Energy and volume can be decoupled: whispered urgency = high energy, low volume
    - Layer 1 descriptors: "low energy", "high energy", "urgent", "subdued", "intense", "measured intensity"

    **## Expressiveness**
    - Define expressiveness as the range of emotional variation across the delivery
    - Spectrum: flat/deadpan (minimal variation) → natural conversational → animated → exaggerated
    - Deadpan is a valid and deliberate target (meme-quotes category often uses it for irony)
    - High expressiveness = wide pitch and timing swings, strong emotional colouring
    - Layer 1 descriptors: "deadpan", "flat", "expressive", "animated", "exaggerated", "naturalistic", "restrained"

    **## Pause & Breath**
    - Define pauses and breath as structural tools, not just gaps
    - Types: micro-pause (beat, comma-length), dramatic pause (emphasis before key word), breath-as-rhythm (audible inhale as pacing beat)
    - Breath humanises AI voice — lack of breath is a primary AI tell
    - Pause placement affects perceived confidence: pausing before the key word (not after) = authority

    **## Warmth vs Authority**
    - Define the spectrum: warmth = intimacy, relatability, softness; authority = credibility, certainty, directness
    - Not a binary — most effective voices blend both
    - Warmth-leaning: conversational, lower energy, slightly slower, breath-present
    - Authority-leaning: clear enunciation, consistent register, deliberate pacing, minimal upward inflection
    - Layer 1 descriptors: "warm", "intimate", "authoritative", "credible", "direct", "conversational"

    **## Anti-AI Patterns**
    - List the primary AI voice tells with their script-level fixes:
    - Even pacing — Fix: vary sentence length; use short punchy sentences next to longer flowing ones
    - Monotone pitch — Fix: write with punctuation that forces pitch change (questions, exclamations, ellipses)
    - No breath — Fix: write breath beats into the script using commas, dashes, or [pause] markers
    - Robotic rhythm — Fix: use contractions, fragments, and natural spoken language rhythm rather than formal prose
    - Consistent energy throughout — Fix: design energy arc into the script (opener high, middle settles, CTA lifts again)
    - Upward inflection on every statement — Fix: use periods deliberately; avoid ending declarative sentences with a rising lilt
